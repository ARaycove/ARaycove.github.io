Academic Plan

Introduction:

I am a non-traditional student coming back to school after almost a decade in order to change career paths. I've been driving a truck for the last 5 years and because of it my social and mental well being has suffered greatly. However during this time I rediscovered the joy of learning through audiobooks, as a result I was able to explore other possibilities through the great world of books. They're a window into a wider world for those who can't experience it themselves. So I learned about the wider world and how I fit into it, I explored the meaning of life and subsequently explored the pointlessness of it all. Yet through that experience I regained what I once had as a child, a great curiosity about everything under the sun and beyond it, and most importantly an idealism that the world doesn't have to be what it is. It can be better and we can do something about it. We're not destined to continue old patterns.

Doing something about it:

It's not enough for me to just think about it, I need to do something about it. From the driver's seat of truck, there's not much you can do. I've tried a lot of things and failed, but that's OK. So this time I'm choosing to get a more formal education and perhaps find people I can connect with. My primary goal here is to learn and find a network of people that I can work with to mold a brighter future with. And most importantly help others along the way in whatever capacity I can.

I believe strongly that I will eventually find a position in the public sector serving in an academic settings. Much of civic policy is determined by the research of academics, so it serves as a way to make real change.

Networking

During my time at university I hope to develop my communication skills, and network with as many people as I can. While this is out of my comfort zone, I believe it to be essential to my plans.

To further this part of my plan, I am vying for the president position of the computer science club. This would allow me to network with faculty and work with them to provide opportunities to my fellow CS majors. In addition to operating a club. I will also be seeking relevant internships to further develop my skills and network.

Specializing:

Ideals only get you so far, so I'm choosing to specialize in the area of Computer Science as I believe it to be the most powerful and versatile skill-set to achieve my more "grander" visions. It's my belief that becoming especially skilled in this field will allow me to interface with experts in every field. However I do understand I have much to learn, after initial assessments it seems I've forgotten a great deal of things from High School, most notably I need to learn a great deal of mathematics and I'm starting over at basic algebra. So while this is a great hurdle, I plan to take summer classes and employ my newfound ability to self-study topics to "catch up" so I have time to take the higher level mathematics courses that will aid in my mastery of my chosen field.

Personal Development:

The major I have chosen is rather rigid in the course requirements, so a large bulk of the classes selected are directly related and it leaves very little room for other interests. Given I have rather grand ambitions, there are a great deal of skills and knowledge I must learn as well. Ranking at the top of this list is an understanding of Public speaking, Leadership, Writing, Psychology, Sociology, Anthropology, and History. These don't fit in my major, and there is only limited space to fit some of this into my first four years. I have already been reading a great deal in my spare time on these topics, and I look forward to the opportunity to audit some of these classes. Despite the fact that I would not get credit for auditing classes in these fields I will still get to learn and gain expertise across the a wider spectrum. This will assist me in being able to achieve more in life.

What Does it mean to be honorable?

A Short Socratic Dialogue

Three men hike into the woods on a weekend trip, after many miles into the wilderness they make camp. Cedric, Oliver, Eli pull the tents off their backs, they dig a fire-pit, set up the cooking gear, light the fires, pitch the tents, set their chairs down and rest a while. Eli pulls a smorgasbord of food out and makes a fine feast for him and his friends. They all begin to eat without a word, the food is delicious. Cedric misses his mouth, food spread across his beard and Oliver offers a tissue. After the meal finishes Cedric loudly proclaims that is was the best meal he's had in a while. Oliver nods in agreement.

Cedric: That was mighty honorable of you Eli, I was not expecting such a thing out here.

Eli: Thank you Cedric, I always try to do what I can for my friends.

Oliver: Cedric, that's a fine thing to say but what is honor actually?

Cedric: Honor is to do well to one's friends!

Oliver: Don't some do well to their friends only to serve themselves in the end?

Cedric: I suppose some do, but perhaps not many.

Oliver: True, there are not many of these, but there are some. Wouldn't such people not be considered to be honorable though, such a person only does the things to they to serve themselves with no regard to others.

Cedric: I would say so, we do care about the intentions of others.

Oliver: So it is only honorable to do well to one's friends if your intentions are not deceitful.

Cedric: Sure, that's a better way to put it.

Eli: But intentions aren't definitive enough for that I would say, there is a saying that the road to hell is paved by good intentions. So I would say that we could have good intentions yet do harm to our friends, and do good to our friends with bad intentions. So if I do harm to you both but my intentions are pure am I still honorable?

Oliver: That's hard to say

Eli: And there are those that do great good to the world with the most vile of intentions yet do the world great justice. A scientist seeking glory and fame could produce a discovery so profound that it benefits humanity for millennia to come. So good can be done with or without intentions.

Cedric: You raise a good point, perhaps good deeds and the intentions behind them don't relate to honor in and of themselves. So what then say you that honor is?

Eli: Honor is to be loyal to one's group, to be just and fair among the community in which they belong. We know the phrase honor among thieves and thieves are not generally good people, and we ourselves would call our soldiers good and honorable despite the fact that they kill others. Yet I think we can agree that killing another human being is not a good thing. I don't think that good ethics and morality in necessarily tied to Honor even though it is virtue to be honorable.

Cedric: What then would you say to a person that leaves their family, their wife and children never to see them again and come here to America to provide a wealth back home? He harms his family yet helps them with monetary gain. Is he honorable?

Eli: I suppose then we would need to explore what loyalty and justice is then. On one hand he remains loyal by helping his family, but harms his children by depriving them of a parent. It's hard to say whether he is really helping them. Yet I would say he is honorable despite the morally ambiguous situation he's in.

Oliver: But what of loyalty? Isn't to be loyal to be consistent with one's values?

Eli: Indeed!

Oliver: And would we call a liar loyal?

Eli: I think we could, it would depend on why he is lying.

Oliver: So intentions are a part of honor?

Eli: Perhaps to some extent, but no if he is lying to protect the interests of his group then he is loyal to them. If he is lying to protect himself even though he may harm others than he is loyal only to himself.

Oliver: So then honor is to do well by one's community and to protect their interests?

Eli: No, that was the first answer and we agreed that doing well to one's friends, while that is virtuous is not quite what honor is. So what then is it?

And so the night went on and the fire died down, the three went on discussing the matter for a couple hours more. They never did come up with a definitive answer, but the subject was much clearer than when they began.
